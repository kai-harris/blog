Do you put in as much effort when nobody is looking as when a coach / boss / colleague / spouse isn't looking over your shoulder?

Seth Godin nails it in his post Self Directed Effort is the Best Kind.  There is an entire industry setup to tell you that you can't put in the required effort without external forces.  Do you really need rewards or punishment or cajoling to put forth your best effort?

Changing behavior to direct your own level of effort can be done is a series of small decisions that turn into a lifestyle and a mentality.  Don't hit snooze, do an extra 5-10 minutes of focused work between breaks, run one last wind sprint when it seems you can't do another.  Lose the one thing that most distracts you during the day.

Matter over Mind - small changes to your behavior will ultimately also change your mindset and it becomes part of you.
