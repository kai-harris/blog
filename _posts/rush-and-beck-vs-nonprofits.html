Peter Campbell nicely summarizes nonprofit responses to the current attacks on nonprofits employees as "lazy idiots" and "rapists" of the economy by Rush Limbaugh, including Robert Egger's great video response.

Quoted from Peter's excellent post:
It’s completely unintuitive and bizarre that nonprofits—poorly resourced organizations that struggle to do the work that our government does less and less of—are lambasted and threatened by the people that rally loudest for eliminating government programs.  We’re the ones who are getting important work done with funding that is volunteered, not assessed.  Most nonprofits have no leftist or rightwing agenda—they have clearly stated missions that they’re trying to serve (as Egger’s video makes clear).  Maybe Beck and Limbaugh should be a bit more appreciative of the fact that we enable mouths to be fed, museums and parks to stay open, and air to continue to be breathable in a country where the government can get fewer and fewer social services funded.

Some statistics:
  • Nonprofits reported over $1.4 trillion in total revenue and nearly $1.3 trillion in expenses in 2007.  They also reported nearly $2.6 trillion in total assets. (source)
  • Nonprofits account for over 8% of all wages and salaries paid in the US in 2006. (source)
  • Nonprofits are not just touchy-feely social service organizations - Churches, Synagogues, and Mosques; the Boy and Girl Scouts, universities; hospitals; museums; bowling leagues; Parent Teacher Associations;  and Veterans' organizations are all examples of nonprofits
